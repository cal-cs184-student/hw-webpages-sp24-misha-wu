Part 1: Bezier Curves with 1D de Casteljau Subdivision

In this part, we use a 1D version of de Casteljau Subdivision to evaluate bezier curves.

The de Casteljau algorithm is a method of interpolating a smooth (C2 continuity) curve via recursive linear interpolation (LERPs) between each iteration of points, terminating when there is only one final point after interpolation. In this case, because we only evaluate one step, we only perform a series of LERPs on alternating control points.

As you can see, this curve retains its continuity, even if you move the control points or adjust the t parameter.

Part 3: Area-Weighted Vertex Normals

Next, we wish to smoothly interpolate the shading in our triangle meshes.

We do this by defining the normal() of a Vertex object as the approximation of the unit normal at this point. To do this, we take the sum of the surrounding faces‚Äô normal vectors.

To find the normal vector, we can take the cross product of two triangle sides; because we are normalizing at the end, this resulting cross product already weighs the vector by the underlying area.

Finally, we normalize to a unit vector at the end, getting our area-weighted vertex normal.

Calculating this allows us to implement Phong shading, giving us smoother-looking curved faces.
